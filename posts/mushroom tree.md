![[mushroom-tree.jpg|Two large shelf fungi at the base of a tree.]]

Happened across these striking mushrooms erupting from a dying tree on a morning walk back in May. I believe they're *Cerioporus squamosus*, common name "dryad's saddle" or "pheasant back". 

Absolutely obsessed with the range of colors, from the palest off-white cream to those deep red-brown scaly edges. I actually pulled several colors directly from this photo for my website palette. 

Every time I look at this photo I think the mushrooms look like hands reaching out from the trunk. That might be a morbid thought, since the tree itself is dead or dying, and the fungus is another organism inhabiting its shell, mycelium honeycombing through the wood, fruiting bodies reaching out through the bark into the sunlight and air.

Even so, there's something compellingly beautiful about fungus. It reminds me of [[decay is an extant form of life|that mushroom post that haunts so many of us]].
